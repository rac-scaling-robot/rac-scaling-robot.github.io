RaC: Robot Learning for Long-Horizon Tasks by Scaling Recovery and Correction

Zheyuan Hu1, Robyn Wu1, Naveen Enock1, Jasmine Li1, Riya Kadakia1, Zackory Erickson1, Aviral Kumar1
1Carnegie Mellon University;
arXiv Code (Coming Soon)
RaC achieves more efficient performance scaling via a simple human-in-the-loop "mid-training" phase added after imitation pre-training. It standardizes human intervention data collection protocols with two rules:
  1. Upon intervention, operators first recover back to in-distribution states, then perform correction to complete the sub-task.
  2. After intervention completes, operator terminates the episode to prevent further rollouts.
Training on these recovery & correction rich segments teaches policies to reset, retry, and adapt, mitigating compounding errors in long-horizon, contact-rich tasks.
